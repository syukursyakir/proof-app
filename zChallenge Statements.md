## 🧩 Challenge Statements

Choose one of the four themes. Each theme has 5–7 specific challenge statements to pick from.

#### Theme A — AI Operating Layer: Trust, Agency & Real-World Systems

1. **Trust and permissions for autonomous agents** — How might we design permission layers, audit trails, and fail-safes so users can trust agents to act on their behalf?
2. **Authenticity in a synthetic world** — How might we build lightweight provenance tools that help people verify what is real, human, and trustworthy?
3. **Human agency in AI-mediated decisions** — How might we help people understand, override, and shape AI-mediated decisions in everyday life?
4. **From prompt user to system architect** — How might we help young people move from using AI casually to building AI-powered systems that compound their capability?
5. **AI for the physical world** — How might we build AI systems that work reliably in messy, real-world environments where failure has physical consequences?
6. **Trust, Commerce & Fraud** — How might we build systems that make online transactions safer, detect scams earlier, and help users decide who or what to trust?
7. **Security, Resilience & Defence** — How might we build AI systems that strengthen national, organisational, and individual resilience while keeping humans accountable?

#### Theme B — Work, Income & Capability

1. **The broken bridge from learning to earning** — How might we help young people turn demonstrated ability into income, projects, or credible opportunities faster?
2. **Entry-level work is being compressed** — How might we create new pathways for students and early-career workers to build judgement, taste, and real-world experience?
3. **AI teammates for small teams** — How might we build AI teammates that help small organisations perform work they could not otherwise afford?
4. **Selling outcomes, not software** — How might we build products that deliver a measurable job-to-be-done, such as resolving a ticket or qualifying a lead?
5. **Opportunity access is socially gated** — How might we redesign opportunity discovery so access is driven less by social capital and more by demonstrated potential?

#### Theme C — AI for Good: Public Benefit, Access & Inclusion

1. **Public services that do not sleep** — How might we build AI assistants that make public or social services easier to access, while preserving human escalation?
2. **AI for underserved learners** — How might we build AI tools that help underserved learners receive personalised support without widening inequality?
3. **Civic trust and misinformation resilience** — How might we help communities detect manipulation, verify information, and rebuild trust in public communication?
4. **Accessibility and independent living** — How might AI support communication, mobility, memory, safety, or care coordination without being invasive?
5. **Community problem-solving with AI** — How might we help neighbourhoods, schools, or nonprofits use AI to identify problems, coordinate volunteers, and measure impact?

#### Theme D — Health, Longevity & Sustainability

1. **Preventive health feels inaccessible** — How might we make preventive health and longevity actionable, affordable, and embedded into everyday routines?
2. **Unsafe experimentation in health optimisation** — How might we support safe, evidence-aware health experimentation without pushing users into misinformation?
3. **Poor fit of Western health defaults in Asian contexts** — How might we build health systems that are adapted to Asian genetics, diets, climates, and work cultures?
4. **Hidden burnout among caregivers** — How might we better support caregivers' energy, resilience, and mental health before burnout becomes chronic?
5. **Health literacy starts too late** — How might we help young people make informed long-term health decisions earlier, using empowering rather than fear-based systems?


Challenge-Solution Fit (20%) 	1. Does the project meaningfully solve the challenge statement? Does it solve a meaningful problem or clearly improve a real-world task?
2. Is the target audience or use case well-defined?
3. Does the solution directly address the core bottleneck, not just symptoms?
AI Leverage and Technological Execution (25%) 	1. Does the core product work reliably during the demo?
2. Are APIs integrated in a way that adds real value (not superficial usage)?
3. Is there evidence of custom logic, workflows, or system design (not just boilerplate)?
4. Is the architecture sensible for the problem (data flow, modularity, etc.)?
5. Does the solution go beyond a simple wrapper around an LLM?
Product Thinking & UI/UX (20%) 	1. Is the user flow and interface clear, intuitive, and easy to navigate?
2. Does the product reduce friction vs current alternatives?
3. Is the output/actionable value obvious to the user?
4. Does the solution feel like a complete experience, not just a feature?
Originality & Insight (20%) 	1. Is the approach differentiated from typical solutions?
2. Does it reframe the problem in an interesting way?
3. Does it feel like something that could evolve into a strong product?
Evidence of Real Demand (15%)
(Not expected, but would be a differentiator) 	1. Did the team speak to users (interviews, surveys, quick validation)?
2. Do they show evidence of:
1. pain points
2. user behaviour
3. real workflows
3. Have they validated assumptions, even lightly?
4. Do they demonstrate insight beyond guesswork?
