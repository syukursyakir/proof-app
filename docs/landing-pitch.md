## Section 2 — The problem (Theme B framing)
**Small employers hire on the wrong signals.**
No recruiting team, no time. So screening comes down to resumes, referrals, and gut feel — which means hiring runs on *social capital, not skill*. Good candidates with the wrong network get filtered out. And every candidate gets a different interview on a different day from an interviewer in a different mood.

**The cost:** ~3 in 4 small businesses say they've made a bad hire, at an average of **$14,900** each (CareerBuilder). Meanwhile in Singapore there are **1.58 job openings per jobseeker** (MOM, 2025) — small employers are losing the talent war on gut feel. Structured, consistent interviews predict performance **~2× better** than gut calls (McDaniel et al.).

## Section 3 — How it works (3 steps)
1. **Speak the role.** Describe your ideal hire out loud. Clarion turns it into a structured rubric + interview in minutes. (OpenAI Whisper + GPT)
2. **Candidate does one voice interview.** An AI interviewer runs the same conversation for every candidate — adaptive follow-ups, and it answers their questions honestly, including "how am I being judged?" (ElevenLabs Conversational AI — *the hero*)
3. **Get the evidence.** A verdict scored against your rubric, where every judgment links to the exact transcript quote that earned it. **You** advance or reject.

## Section 4 — Why Clarion (4 pillars)
- **Consistent, not moody.** Same questions, same scoring, every candidate. Not "how the interviewer felt that day."
- **Skill over social capital.** Judged on what they demonstrate, not who they know.
- **Transparent to both sides.** The employer sees the evidence; the candidate knows what they're assessed on.
- **Built for small teams.** No enterprise contract, no recruiter required.

## Section 5 — The glass box (the differentiator)
**Most AI hiring tools score you in a black box. Clarion shows its work.**
Every score expands to the transcript quote behind it. The AI assesses; the human decides. Show a verdict card: criterion → score → justification → highlighted quote.

## Section 6 — Closing CTA

## Visual & brand direction (for the dev)
- **Vibe:** clean, confident, trustworthy — fintech-grade, not playful. Lots of whitespace.
- **Theme:** dark hero (makes the orb glow), light content sections below.
- **Accent:** electric violet/blue for the orb + CTAs (#6D5EF8-ish). One accent only.
- **Type:** Geist or Inter. Big confident H1.
- **Motion:** the orb is the only animated element on the landing — subtle idle pulse. Don't over-animate.
- **Don't build:** nav-heavy chrome, pricing tables, testimonials, footers with 40 links. One screen, one story, two CTAs.

## The wedge (for the deck "why now / why us" slide)
Incumbents force small employers to pick between **a cheap recording tool with no scoring** (Hireflix, Willo) and **an expensive black-box enterprise scorer with legal baggage** (HireVue — dropped facial analysis after an FTC complaint; Workday — facing a nationwide AI-bias class action; Maki — enterprise-only, no public pricing). **No one offers an affordable, self-serve, *explainable* AI voice interviewer with a human-in-the-loop for small employers.** That's Clarion's lane. (ElevenLabs is confirmed in production only at Bolna + Maki — both built for agencies/enterprise, not founders. The small-employer voice interview is open white space — our ElevenLabs-bonus angle.)
