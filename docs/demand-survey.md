# Clarion — Evidence-of-Demand Survey Pack

Two short surveys that *quantify* demand for the hackathon's "Evidence of Real
Demand" criterion (15%). The interview guide in `evidence-of-demand.md` gives you
the **qualitative** quotes; this gives you the **numbers** to put on a slide.

**Two-sided on purpose:** Clarion is a marketplace. You want to show jobseekers
*want* a fairer shot **and** small employers will *pay* for better/fairer hiring.

---

## How to use (≈10 min setup)

1. Create **two** Google Forms: "Clarion — Jobseeker survey" and "Clarion —
   Hiring survey". (Typeform works too.)
2. Paste each section below. Question types are tagged `[Multiple choice]`,
   `[Checkboxes]`, `[Linear scale]`, `[Short answer]`, `[Paragraph]`.
3. Turn ON "Collect email addresses → Do not collect" (keep it anonymous for
   honesty) — except the optional email-for-early-access question.
4. Mark only the ⭑ questions as **Required**. Fewer required = higher completion.
5. Send via the blurbs at the bottom. Target **15–25 responses per side** — more
   than enough to show a signal for a hackathon.
6. ~24h before submission, screenshot the Forms "Responses → Summary" charts and
   drop the key numbers on your demand slide.

> Keep each survey to **<3 minutes**. The versions below are ~9–11 questions.
> If you only have time for one channel, prioritise the **Jobseeker** survey —
> it maps directly to Theme B5 (opportunity access is socially gated).

# SURVEY A — Jobseekers / Candidates

*Form title:* **Clarion — Are you judged by your resume or your ability?**
*Form description:* "2-minute, anonymous. We're researching how fair hiring is
for people without a 'brand-name' background. Your honest answers help."

### Section 1 — About you

**Q1 ⭑ Which best describes you right now?** `[Multiple choice]`
- Secondary / JC student
- Polytechnic / ITE student
- University student
- Working — less than 2 years' experience
- Working — 2+ years' experience
- Currently job hunting / between jobs

**Q2 Your age range** `[Multiple choice]`
- Under 18 · 18–21 · 22–25 · 26–34 · 35+

### Section 2 — The pain

**Q3 ⭑ In the last 2 years, roughly how many jobs or internships have you applied to?** `[Multiple choice]`
- 0 · 1–5 · 6–15 · 16–40 · 40+

**Q4 ⭑ How often were you rejected or ghosted *without any interview or test* — judged on your resume/CV alone?** `[Linear scale 1–5]`
- 1 = Never → 5 = Almost every time

**Q5 ⭑ Have you ever felt you could have done a job well, but never got the chance to prove it?** `[Multiple choice]`
- Yes, often · Yes, sometimes · No, not really

**Q6 What do you think hurts your applications the most?** `[Checkboxes]`
- I didn't go to a "brand-name" school
- Limited work experience
- No connections / referrals
- Resume formatting / not passing the ATS software
- Gaps in my CV
- My background (age, nationality, etc.)
- Not sure
- Other…

### Section 3 — The solution

**Q7 ⭑ Imagine an employer let you prove your ability with a short skills test + interview *instead of* judging your resume first. How appealing is that?** `[Linear scale 1–5]`
- 1 = Not at all → 5 = Extremely appealing

**Q8 ⭑ Which would you rather be judged on?** `[Multiple choice]`
- My resume and background
- A fair, skills-based assessment
- Depends on the job

### Section 4 — Willingness + the quote

**Q9 ⭑ Would you spend ~30 minutes on a skills assessment if it gave you a real shot regardless of your background?** `[Multiple choice]`
- Yes, definitely · Probably · Only for jobs I really want · No

**Q10 Tell us about a time you were judged by your resume or background instead of your actual ability.** `[Paragraph]`
*(This is your quote goldmine for the deck — leave it optional so people open up.)*

**Q11 Want early access? Drop your email (optional).** `[Short answer]`

# SURVEY B — Small employers / Hiring

*Form title:* **Clarion — How do you hire, and what does it cost you?**
*Form description:* "2-minute, anonymous. For anyone who hires or helps hire,
especially at smaller companies. Researching the cost of getting hiring wrong."

### Section 1 — About you (screener)

**Q1 ⭑ Do you hire or help hire at your organisation?** `[Multiple choice]`
- Yes — I make hiring decisions
- I influence / am involved in hiring
- No *(→ thank-you, end — keeps your data clean)*

**Q2 ⭑ Company size** `[Multiple choice]`
- Just me / founder · 2–10 · 11–50 · 51–200 · 200+

**Q3 Roughly how many people do you hire per year?** `[Multiple choice]`
- 0–2 · 3–10 · 11–30 · 30+

### Section 2 — The pain

**Q4 ⭑ How do you screen candidates today?** `[Checkboxes]`
- Resume / CV review
- Referrals & network
- Phone / video screens
- Skills tests or work samples
- An ATS / recruiting software
- Gut feel
- Other…

**Q5 ⭑ How confident are you that your process picks the *best* candidate — not just the best resume?** `[Linear scale 1–5]`
- 1 = Not confident → 5 = Very confident

**Q6 ⭑ Your biggest hiring headache?** `[Checkboxes]`
- Too many applicants to screen
- Hard to judge real ability from a resume
- Bad hires are expensive
- Hiring takes too long
- Bias / fairness / legal concerns
- Can't compete with big firms for talent
- Other…

**Q7 Have you ever hired someone who looked great on paper but underperformed?** `[Multiple choice]`
- Yes, multiple times · Yes, once · No

### Section 3 — The solution

**Q8 ⭑ How valuable would a tool be that gives *every* applicant a fair skills + interview assessment and a transparent, evidence-backed scorecard?** `[Linear scale 1–5]`
- 1 = Not valuable → 5 = Extremely valuable

**Q9 ⭑ What matters most to you in hiring?** `[Multiple choice]`
- Saving time · Hiring better people · Being fair & legally defensible · Lower cost

### Section 4 — Willingness to pay (the revenue story)

**Q10 ⭑ Would you try a tool like this for your next hire?** `[Multiple choice]`
- Yes · Maybe · No

**Q11 ⭑ What would you pay to assess *one role* (unlimited candidates)?** `[Multiple choice]`
- I'd only use it free
- $1–20 · $21–50 · $51–100 · $100+

**Q12 What's the hardest part of hiring for you right now?** `[Paragraph]`

**Q13 Want early access? Drop your email (optional).** `[Short answer]`

## How to read the results for your deck

Pull these onto **one demand slide** (a couple of numbers per side beats a wall
of charts):

**Candidate demand**
- "**__%** of jobseekers have been rejected/ghosted on their resume alone
  (4–5 on Q4)." ← the gating problem, in one number
- "**__%** said a skills-first assessment is appealing (4–5 on Q7)."
- "**__%** would do a 30-min assessment for a fair shot (Q9 Yes/Probably)."
- One **verbatim quote** from Q10. ← humanises the slide

**Employer demand**
- "**__%** aren't confident their process picks the best candidate (1–3 on Q5)."
- "**__%** have made a 'great on paper' bad hire (Q7)."
- "**__%** would try it for their next hire (Q10 Yes/Maybe)."
- "**__%** would pay for it (Q11, anything above free)." ← willingness-to-pay = revenue proof

**The headline:** both sides feel the pain → two-sided demand → Clarion sits in
the middle. That's the story the 15% rubric line is looking for.

> Tip: even n=15–20 per side is a legitimate "we validated this" signal for a
> hackathon. State your sample size honestly on the slide — judges respect it.
