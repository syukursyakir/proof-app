# Clarion — Full Per-Person Interview Scripts

Word-for-word scripts you can read top-to-bottom for each of the 4 interviewees.
Pairs with `evidence-of-demand.md` (the why) and `demand-survey.md` (the numbers).

**Read naturally — don't recite.** ~15 min each. Rules:
- **Discovery before demo.** Ask about their *real past*, don't pitch (Mom Test).
- **Pause after the ★ questions.** Count to 3. Silence pulls the quote.
- **80% them, 20% you.** Chase the emotional moments. Write the exact words down.
- `[brackets]` = stage directions / what to listen for. Don't read those out.

---

## 1 · Polytechnic / ITE student  *(candidate lens)*

**Set up (30s)**
> "Hey, thanks for doing this. I'm researching how people actually get hired —
> internships, first jobs, all that — because I think the system's kind of unfair
> to people without the right background. No right answers, I just want your real
> experiences. Mind if I take a few notes while we talk?"

**Discovery (~10 min)**
> "So first — what are you studying, and have you done any internships or
> part-time work yet?"  [let them set the scene]
>
> "Tell me about the last competitive thing you applied for — an internship, a
> part-time job, a scholarship, even a CCA leadership spot. Walk me through it."
> [probe: *How did they choose people? What do you think it actually came down to?*]
>
> "When you applied — did you write your CV or portfolio yourself, or use a
> template or AI? Was there any online test? Did you even hear back?"
> [listen for: ghosting, ATS filters, templated CVs]
>
> "Do you feel your grades and transcript really capture what you're good at?
> Like — what are you genuinely good at that just doesn't show up on paper?"
>
> ★ "Have you ever felt you could totally nail a role, but had no way to prove it
> on paper — so you never got the chance? Tell me about that time."  [PAUSE]
>
> "When you picture applying for your first real job after you graduate — what
> worries you most?"
>
> "Be honest — do you think who you know, or which school you're from, matters
> more than what you can actually do? How does that feel?"

**Show the app (3 min)**
> "Can I show you the thing I'm building? 60 seconds."  [demo: pick a role →
> candidate does the 3-part assessment → glass-box verdict with the /100 + evidence]
>
> "Would something like this have helped in that internship situation you told me
> about?"
> "As a candidate — would you rather do this, or fire off another resume?"
> "What's missing? What would stop you using it?"

**Wrap (90s)**
> "Last thing — would you actually use this if a company offered it? Would you
> tell a friend who's job-hunting? … This was super helpful, thank you. Could you
> also do this 2-min survey so I've got it on record?"  [send jobseeker link]

## 2 · JC student  *(candidate lens — youngest, mostly studies)*

> Anchor on selection experiences they HAVE had (grades, admissions, scholarships,
> CCA roles) and the *feeling* of being judged on paper. Don't assume job history.

**Set up (30s)**
> "Thanks for chatting! I'm looking into how people get judged when they apply for
> things — jobs, courses, scholarships. Your honest take matters even if you
> haven't worked yet; your experience with grades and applications totally counts.
> Mind if I jot some notes?"

**Discovery (~10 min)**
> "What level are you at now, and what are you hoping to do after JC?"
>
> "Have you ever applied for something competitive where you got picked — or
> didn't? A scholarship, a CCA leadership role, a uni or course application, a
> part-time gig? Tell me about one."  [probe: *How were people chosen?*]
>
> "Do you feel your grades actually capture what you're good at? What are you good
> at that grades don't show?"
>
> ★ "Have you ever felt you'd be great at something, but there was no way to prove
> it — you just had to hope your results spoke for you? Tell me about it."  [PAUSE]
>
> "When you think about applying for internships or your first job in a few years
> — what worries you most about how they'll judge you?"
>
> "Do you think your school name and your grades will matter more than what you
> can actually do? How does that sit with you?"

**Show the app (3 min)**
> "Here's what I'm building — imagine this is how you applied for your first
> internship, instead of them just looking at your transcript."  [60s demo]
>
> "Would you rather be judged like this, or just on your results?"
> "Does this feel fairer, or does anything about it worry you?"
> "What's missing?"

**Wrap (90s)**
> "Would you use this when the time comes? Would you tell a friend? … Thank you so
> much — could you do this quick 2-min survey too?"  [send jobseeker link]

## 3 · Working adult who HIRES  *(employer lens — dig hardest, most valuable)*

**Set up (30s)**
> "Thanks for the time. I'm researching how smaller companies actually hire —
> because the proper tools out there are all built for big firms. I just want the
> real story, warts and all. Mind if I take notes?"

**Discovery (~10 min)**
> "First — do you make hiring decisions, or help decide? Roughly how big's the
> team, and how often are you hiring?"
>
> "Walk me through *exactly* how you hired the last person who joined. Start from
> the job opening."  [let them tell the whole story — don't interrupt]
>
> "How did you decide who to interview — and then who to actually hire?"
> [listen for: resume, referral, gut feel]
>
> "When you look at resumes now, how much do you actually trust them? Have you
> noticed they all kind of look the same lately — like they're AI-written?"
>
> ★ "Have you ever hired someone who looked great on paper and then it didn't work
> out? … What did that cost you — time, money, the team's morale?"  [PAUSE]
>
> "How long does hiring usually take you, and what's the single most painful part?"
>
> "Have you ever used any hiring tools — an ATS, skills tests, those one-way video
> interviews? Why, or why not?"
>
> "When you honestly can't tell who's good, what do you fall back on?"

**Show the app (3 min)**
> "Here's what I'm building — 60 seconds."  [demo: describe a role by voice →
> candidate self-serves the assessment → you get a ranked, evidence-backed scorecard]
>
> "Would this have helped with that hire that didn't work out?"
> "Would you actually try it for your next hire?"
> ★ "And honestly — what would you pay to assess one role, unlimited candidates?
> Ballpark, don't be shy."  [write the number down]
> "What's missing? What would stop you?"

**Wrap (90s)**
> "Would you try it for real? Would you recommend it to another owner/manager? …
> Really appreciate this — could you do this 2-min survey so it's on record?"
> [send employer link]

[Capture: their real workflow ("I just skim and go with gut"), the bad-hire cost,
the would-pay number, and one verbatim quote.]

## 4 · Working adult as candidate  *(candidate lens — has job-hunted)*

**Set up (30s)**
> "Thanks for doing this. I'm researching how people get hired, and how fair (or
> not) it feels from the applicant's side. Just want your honest experience — mind
> if I take notes?"

**Discovery (~10 min)**
> "Tell me about your last proper job hunt. What was the most frustrating part?"
>
> "How do you write your resume these days — do you use AI, tailor keywords to get
> past the filters?"  [listen for: ATS-gaming, ChatGPT'd CVs]
>
> "Have you ever had to do an online assessment, or one of those one-way video
> interviews where you talk to a screen? What was that like?"
> [listen for: black-box test pain, no feedback]
>
> ★ "Ever felt you were judged on your CV or your background instead of what you
> can actually do? Tell me about a specific time."  [PAUSE]
>
> "Did companies ever just leave you hanging — no reply, no feedback at all? How
> did that feel?"
>
> "If you could change one thing about how you were assessed in a job hunt, what
> would it be?"

**Show the app (3 min)**
> "Here's what I'm building — 60 seconds."  [demo]
>
> "Would this have helped in that job hunt you described?"
> "Would you rather do this once, or keep firing resumes into the void?"
> "What's missing? What would stop you?"

**Wrap (90s)**
> "Would you actually use this? Would you tell a friend who's job-hunting? …
> Thank you — could you do this quick 2-min survey too?"  [send jobseeker link]

## After every interview (do it in the 30s before you forget)

Log in your notes / `evidence-of-demand.md`:
- **Persona + 1-line context**
- **Best verbatim quote** (exact words)
- **What they actually do today** (real workflow / behaviour)
- **Pain** + how often / how costly  ·  **Reaction** (did they lean in? what did they ask for unprompted?)
- Employer only: **would-pay number**

**The money shot for the deck:** put a hirer's quote
(*"I just go with my gut — and I've been burned"*) next to a student's
(*"I know I could do the job, I just never get the interview"*). Those two lines,
side by side, ARE the B5 thesis — validated by real people.
