# Clarion — Outreach & Interview Script

What to actually *say* when you approach someone — in person, on a call, or as a
voice note. Two variants (jobseeker / employer). Read it naturally, don't recite.
Goal: get the survey filled **and** capture 1–2 verbatim quotes for the deck.

**Golden rules**
- Don't pitch. You're *researching a problem*, not selling Clarion. People are
  honest when they're not being sold to.
- Shut up and listen. After you ask, count to 3. Silence pulls out the real answer.
- Ask "tell me about a time…" not "would you…". Past behaviour > hypotheticals.
- Never lead ("Don't you hate resumes?"). Stay neutral.

---

## VARIANT 1 — Jobseeker (student / early-career)

**Opening (15 sec)**
> "Hey, can I borrow 3 minutes? I'm working on a project about how people get
> hired — specifically how unfair it can be when you don't have a fancy resume or
> the right connections. No right answers, totally anonymous. I just want your
> honest experience."

**Warm-up**
> "So — what are you up to right now, studying or working? And have you applied
> for jobs or internships recently?"

**The pain (let them talk)**
> "When you applied… how did it usually go? Did you actually get to interviews,
> or did a lot of it just go quiet?"
>
> "Has there ever been a job you knew you could've done well — but you never even
> got the chance to show it? … What happened?"   ← *pause. this is the quote.*
>
> "What do you think held your applications back the most?"

**The idea (now you can introduce it)**
> "Here's what I'm building. Instead of an employer judging your resume first,
> you get to actually prove you can do the job — a short skills test and a
> quick interview — and everyone's judged the same way, on evidence, not pedigree.
> … Be honest: does that sound useful, or meh?"

**Willingness**
> "Would you spend like 30 minutes on something like that if it meant a real shot
> regardless of your background? … For any job, or only ones you really want?"

**Close**
> "This is super helpful. Could you do the 2-min survey too so I have it on
> record? [send link] And if you want early access, I'll add your email. Thank you!"

## VARIANT 2 — Employer / business owner / hiring manager

**Opening (15 sec)**
> "Quick favour — I'm researching how smaller companies hire, because the tools
> out there are mostly built for big firms. You hire people, right? Can I ask you
> a few questions? Anonymous, like 3 minutes."

**Warm-up**
> "Roughly how big is the team, and how often are you hiring?"

**The pain (let them vent — they will)**
> "Walk me through how you screen people right now — resumes, referrals, gut?"
>
> "How confident are you that your process actually picks the *best* person, and
> not just the best-looking resume?"
>
> "Have you ever hired someone who looked perfect on paper and then… didn't work
> out? What did that cost you — time, money, the team?"   ← *pause. quote gold.*
>
> "What's the most painful part of hiring for you?"

**The idea**
> "What I'm building gives every applicant the same fair skills test + interview,
> and hands you a transparent scorecard with the evidence behind it — so you spend
> less time screening and make fewer bad bets. … Would something like that be
> valuable to you, or not really?"

**Willingness to pay (ask it plainly — don't flinch)**
> "If it worked — would you actually try it for your next hire? … And honestly,
> what would you pay to assess one role with unlimited candidates? Ballpark."

**Close**
> "Really appreciate it. Mind doing the 2-min survey so I've got it logged?
> [send link] Happy to give you early access if you're keen. Thanks!"

## Voice-note version (if you're sending it async)

Record ~25 seconds, casual:
> "Heyy, random one — I'm building a project to make hiring fairer for people
> without a big-name resume. Could you do this tiny 2-min anonymous survey for me?
> And if you've ever been rejected for a job before anyone even talked to you,
> I'd love to hear that story — just voice-note me back. Means a lot 🙏 [link]"

---

## After each conversation (30 sec — do it immediately)

Jot in `evidence-of-demand.md` or a notes app while it's fresh:
- **Persona + 1-line context**
- **Best verbatim quote** (exact words — for the deck)
- **Pain severity** 1–5 and **would-use / would-pay?**
- **Biggest objection** they raised (judges love that you heard the pushback)

Five of these + the survey charts = a demand slide that actually proves the 15%.
