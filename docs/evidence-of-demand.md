# Evidence of Real Demand — interview guide & framing

For the hackathon "Evidence of Real Demand" criterion (15%). Goal: validate the
**pain**, not whether people like the app. We talk to real users first, show the
product last.

Challenge: **Theme B5 — "Opportunity access is socially gated."** Clarion's bet:
hiring should turn on *demonstrated ability*, not pedigree, network, or a
keyword-stuffed resume.

---

## Why now — the resume is dying as a skill signal (the macro tailwind)

This is the "why this, why now" backdrop. Use it to frame the problem.

- **Resume-based hiring measures pedigree, not ability.** Employers screen on
  school, past employer brands, and keywords — proxies for *social capital*, not
  proof of what someone can do. Good people with the wrong network get filtered
  out before anyone sees their skill (this is B5).
- **ATS made it worse, not better.** Most companies run resumes through an
  Applicant Tracking System that auto-filters on keyword matches. So the game
  became "beat the keyword filter," and candidates keyword-stuff to survive — the
  ATS rewards gaming, not competence, and silently rejects qualified people for
  the "wrong" words.
- **AI resume generators just killed the resume as a signal.** Anyone can now
  generate a flawless, perfectly-tailored, ATS-optimised resume in 30 seconds
  with ChatGPT. Result: resumes are converging — *everyone* looks great on paper.
  When every applicant has an AI-perfected, keyword-matched CV, the resume can no
  longer distinguish the strong candidate from the weak one. The signal is gone.
- **So the only reliable signal left is demonstrated ability** — having someone
  actually *do* a slice of the work. That is exactly what Clarion provides:
  proof, not pedigree; performance, not keywords.

> One-liner for the deck: *"AI made every resume look perfect — so resumes no
> longer tell you who can do the job. Clarion measures the one thing AI can't
> fake for a candidate: actually doing the work."*

**Resume-based vs ability-based hiring:**

| | Resume / ATS (today) | Clarion (ability-based) |
|---|---|---|
| What it measures | Keywords, pedigree, network | Demonstrated skill + judgment |
| Gamed by | AI resume generators, keyword-stuffing | Much harder — you have to actually perform |
| Who it favours | Those with the right CV/connections | Whoever can do the job |
| Signal in the AI era | Collapsing (everyone looks identical) | Strengthening |

## Method (read this first)

The rule ("The Mom Test"): **ask about their real past behaviour, not your idea.**
People lie to be nice when you pitch; they tell the truth about what they've
actually done. So:

1. **Discovery first (~10 min):** ask about real, recent experiences. No app yet.
   This is the evidence.
2. **Show the app (~3 min):** quick demo, watch their reaction, ask "would this
   have helped? what's missing?"
3. **Wrap (~2 min):** would you use it / share it / pay for it?

**Why show it last:** demoing first biases every answer toward politeness.
Discovery-first gets you honest pain.

## Who we're talking to (n = 4) and what each validates

Clarion has two users — **employers** (who hire) and **candidates** (who job-seek).
The four interviewees cover both sides of the B5 thesis.

| Interviewee | Lens | Validates |
|---|---|---|
| Polytechnic student | Candidate | Entry-level, judged on grades/connections not skill |
| JC student | Candidate | Youngest, no track record, wants a fair shot |
| Working adult #1 | **Employer** (if they hire) | SMB hires on gut/resume; bad-hire cost |
| Working adult #2 | Candidate | Has job-hunted; resume/ATS/assessment pain |

> If neither adult hires, run both as candidates — still strong evidence.

## Question scripts (discovery — ask about their PAST)

### 🎓 Students (poly + JC) — the socially-gated candidate
1. Tell me about the last time you applied for a job, internship, or part-time
   role. Walk me through it.
2. How do you think the employer decided between you and everyone else?
3. Have you ever felt you'd be *great* at something but never got the chance to
   show it? Tell me about it.
4. Do you feel your school / grades / who you know matters more than what you can
   actually do?
5. Did you write your resume yourself, or use AI / a template? Do you tweak it for
   each application?
6. Have you ever done an online test or assessment to get a job? How did it feel?
   *(listen for: irrelevant, stressful, ghosted)*
7. After applying/testing — did you ever hear back? How did that feel?

### 👔 Working adult who HIRES (the employer) — dig hard, most valuable
1. Walk me through *exactly* how you hired the last person on your team.
2. How did you decide who to interview, and who to actually hire?
   *(listen for: resume, referral, gut)*
3. When you look at resumes now, how much do you trust them? Have you noticed they
   all look kind of the same / AI-written?
4. Have you ever made a hire that didn't work out? What did it cost you — time,
   money, stress?
5. How long does hiring take you, and what's the most painful part?
6. Have you used any hiring or assessment tools (ATS, tests)? Why / why not?
7. When you genuinely can't tell who's good, what do you fall back on?

### 👔 Working adult as candidate (has job-hunted)
1. Tell me about your last job hunt — the most frustrating part?
2. How do you write your resume these days? Do you use AI? Tailor keywords to get
   past filters?
3. Ever had to do an online assessment / one-way video interview? What was that
   like? *(TestGorilla-style pain)*
4. Ever felt judged on your CV or background instead of what you can do?
5. Did companies ever leave you hanging with no feedback?

## Then: show the app (3 min)
Walk them through the golden path quickly: describe/pick a role → candidate does
the 3-part assessment → glass-box verdict with the /100 score and evidence.
Then ask:
- "Would this have helped in the situation you just described?"
- "As a candidate, would you rather do this or send another resume?"
- "What's missing? What would stop you using it?"

Watch for the lean-in moment and what they ask for unprompted.

---

## What to capture (per person)
- **A verbatim quote** (quotes are the most persuasive evidence in the deck).
- **What they actually DO today** (their real workflow — "I just skim resumes and
  go with gut").
- **The pain** + how often / how costly.
- **Reaction to the app** — did they lean in? what did they ask for?
